The PRC views dispatching Chinese talent to international organisations as a key global-governance contribution and a means to shape these institutions from within. Using UN staffing data alongside Chinese-language academic, policy, and media sources, we examine how China prepares to compete for international civil-service posts, finding that its efforts emphasise preparing patriots who can bring &ldquo;Chinese wisdom&rdquo; into the UN, even as overall staffing gains remain in development.'
