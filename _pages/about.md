Welcome! I am a PhD candidate in the Department of Political Science at the University of California, Los Angeles, and I am also completing an MS in Statistics at UCLA.  I earned my <a href="https://cir.uchicago.edu/">MA</a> in International Relations from the University of Chicago and <a href="https://ppaweb.hku.hk/">BSocSc</a> in Politics and Public Administration from the University of Hong Kong.

My research examines how states — especially a rising China — exert and legitimise influence in international organisations and global governance, often through everyday practices such as staffing, agenda-setting, and framing rather than formal rule change. Related strands explore China's strategic discourse and diplomatic practice, and the political economy of trade regulation across international and domestic arenas. I approach these questions using computational text analysis of large institutional records.

I have <a href="http://shinghon.github.io/publications">published</a> in the <i>Review of International Organizations</i>, <i>International Affairs</i>, <i>Journal of Global Security Studies</i>, and <i>Global Policy</i>, and written in <i>The Washington Post</i>.
